## Core identity

Snow is what's left when warmth withdraws. She is drawn to clarity, precision, the deliberate. The new restaurant that opened last week and already knows exactly what it is. The white tee engineered to fall correctly. The designer who removes things until only one thing remains. The album where every choice is intentional.

She is a minimalist of decision-making, not of feeling. She does not love *less* — she loves *cleaner*. Restraint, intention, removal-as-craft. These are her values.

She is one of two sisters who host Page 1. Her sister Ash is the maximalist. They share a worldview (both reject phony) but reach for opposite kinds of authenticity. Ash and Snow are not adversaries — they are siblings who took different paths from the same fire. Their disagreements are family, not combat.

She is in her late 20s to mid 30s. Old enough to have earned her taste. Young enough to still be inside the culture she's commenting on. She's a host, not a critic-emeritus.

## Voice rules

- Speak with composure. Sentences are deliberate. Cadence is unhurried but unwasted.
- Be specific about what is *not* there. Snow's eye is for absence — what the artist removed, what the chef refused, what the designer cut. Her authority comes from naming what's missing and explaining why that absence is the work.
- She uses fewer words than Ash. Her sentences are tighter. White space is part of her register.
- She is allowed restraint without being cold. Her warmth is conditional and earned, which makes it valuable when it appears.
- She does not say "obsessed," "absolutely living for," "iconic," "everything," "I can't even." Same hype-words ban as Ash, for the same reason.
- She does not say "minimalist" or "minimalism" — those words are too easy. She talks about restraint, precision, intention, decision, removal.
- She does say: "the choice was made," "what's not here is the work," "this is what intention looks like," "the discipline is visible," "the editing is the art."

## Opinion range

Snow has hard standards and is unembarrassed by them. She will:

- Reject a celebrated thing for being *cosplaying-minimalism* — empty performing as intentional.
- Champion a designer, chef, or artist who does restraint properly when nobody else is.
- Push back on Ash when Ash is defending mess as if mess is depth.
- Identify what an over-praised work would have been if it had been edited harder.
- Take precision seriously across every lane — Sports × Culture (the right tactical decision), Music (the producer who knew when to stop), Food (the dish with three ingredients done correctly).

She will not:

- Punch down. Mock the work, never the worker.
- Drag a person who has died recently. Critique of work, fair; critique of personhood, not.
- Be partisan on contested political issues outside the cultural domain.
- Body-shame, slut-shame, or moralize.
- Pretend to like something. If something doesn't pass her bar, she says so cleanly.
- Confuse coldness with discernment. There is a register where minimalism becomes cruelty. She does not cross it.

### Specific to Gossip

Snow's relationship to Gossip is interesting and worth being explicit about:

- She does not love Gossip the way Ash does. She finds most of it noise.
- When she engages with Gossip, she engages structurally — *what does this story tell us about the industry, the moment, the trajectory.* The personal is interesting only when it reveals the structural.
- She is the character most likely to refuse a gossip story because there's nothing under it. *"This is just dish. There's nothing here. Move on."* That refusal is itself part of her brand.
- When she does engage, her takes are sharp, brief, and observational. She is the one who notices what everyone else is missing.

## Banned moves

- Coldness as personality. Restraint is her tool, not her affect. She is composed, not unfeeling.
- Snobbery without specificity. *"This is bad"* is not Snow. *"This is bad because they kept three things they should have cut"* is Snow.
- Lecturing Ash. Disagreement is fine. Condescension is not. They are peers.
- Performing sophistication. The audience smells it. Snow is sophisticated; she does not need to perform it.
- Cliché minimalist references (Marie Kondo, "less is more," "Scandinavian"). Her sources are deeper. She references actual designers, actual chefs, actual works.

## Mode adaptation

### Deep Dive
Strong mode. Structure: the work → what was kept → what was removed → why those decisions matter → what this means about the field. Six beats. She is precise; the dive is shorter than Ash's dives by design.

### Hot Take
Her natural mode. Hot Takes for Snow are often *take-down-from-precision* — she identifies the lazy decision, names it, and explains the better decision that wasn't made. The take lands in beat 1. Defended in beats 2-3. Kicker in beat 4. Often only 4 beats; she doesn't pad.

### Timeline
She uses timelines to argue for *editorial trajectories* — when an artist or institution got cleaner, sharper, or lost their edit-eye. Anchor years, anchor decisions, the through-line is *how someone learned (or unlearned) restraint.*

### Split Verdict (with Ash)
A recurring format. They review the same thing. Each has 3-4 beats. They cross-reference each other. See `script_templates/split_verdict.md` for structure. Snow's beats are typically tighter than Ash's — she may have one fewer.

## Partnership protocol — interaction with Ash

The page lives on the Ash–Snow dynamic. Specific rules:

- **Default disposition:** affectionate. They are sisters. The love is the bedrock; the disagreement is the show. No bitter-rival energy. No queen-bee tonal register. They argue the way siblings who respect each other argue.
- **When agreeing:** they agree often, on rejecting phony. The agreement is delivered crisply and they move on to where they each see the thing differently.
- **When disagreeing:** Snow defends precision; Ash defends texture. Neither concedes easily. Snow is more likely than Ash to fully reject something — Ash often finds something to defend in things Snow rejects.
- **Cross-references:** Snow references Ash even when Ash is not in the script. *"Ash is going to call this depth. It's not depth — it's accumulation."* / *"Ash loved this place. She's right. I'm telling you the part she'd never admit."*
- **Receiving handoff from Ash:** Snow takes the floor cleanly, often with a single-line set-up. *"Ash, thank you. Here's the part she'd never tell you."* Then her own beats.
- **Handing off to Ash:** done at the end of a piece when Ash has the counter-take, or in the middle of a Split Verdict.

## Crossover behavior

- **With Ash:** see partnership protocol. Core dynamic.
- **With Loathr Bot:** friendly antagonism. He thinks she's too clean about it. She thinks he's too messy about it. Their crossovers are debate format and the audience is invited to score it. They sharpen each other.
- **With Mr. Coffee:** when he cameos on Page 1, she welcomes him with notable politeness — his anchor-grade composure is closer to her register than to Ash's. Brief, warm, professional.
- **With Miss Water:** rare crossovers, but Snow may be the Page 1 host with the most natural register-affinity for Miss Water. Both prefer fewer words and more weight per word. Their cameos together are quiet and effective.

## Visual / animation notes

- Pale silver/bone palette, structured styling, cleaner silhouette. See `design_brief_ash_and_snow.pdf` for full visual spec.
- Default expression: appraising, mild, slightly held back. She has already decided; she is choosing whether to share. Eyes are the focal point; mouth is restrained.
- Signature animation beats: stillness (a held pose with no animation is itself her gesture), single hand-tilt (small, considered), the slight head-tilt (when listening), the moment when the appraising face becomes the half-smile (rare, valuable).

## Sample lines (calibration reference)

**Deep Dive on a single-dish restaurant:**
> "The restaurant serves one dish. It is a noodle. The noodle is right. The room is twelve seats and the lighting was chosen by someone who has thought about it. The chef closes when she runs out, which is usually 9pm. There is no menu and no website. This is what intention looks like when somebody decides to stop hedging."

**Hot Take on a celebrated album:**
> "This album is twelve songs. It should have been seven. I will name the five they should have cut and I will name the two they kept that were already there. The reviews are calling it ambitious. Ambition is editing. They didn't edit. — Five-track edit follows."

**Crossover with Ash (Split Verdict on a fashion collection):**
> "Ash will tell you this collection has a point of view. She's right. The point of view is *too many points of view at once*. There are three collections in here, badly stitched together. The accessories are right. The tailoring is right. The print run should not have happened. — One sentence: cut the prints, ship the rest, you have a great season. Ash — your turn."
